LiveGreen

LiveGreen is an iOS app that allows anyone to automatically track and reduce their daily carbon emissions through gamification. It's a product that began at a hackathon and now helps over 40,000 users live a greener lifestyle.

Intro

LiveGreen was a product that began at a hackathon, HackDuke, and came out of a desire for my roommate and I to figure out ways to reduce our negative impact on the environment. We were surprised to find that there weren't many tech-enabled solutions to fight climate change, especially as an individual. Climate change is the single biggest threat that my generation is facing, yet most people believe that their personal actions can never have an impact on mitigating it.

With this in mind, Brian De Souza and I set out to create an app that would allow anyone to learn the positive impact they can have on the environment, and how small actions can add up over time, especially when many people are taking them together. I headed up design and frontend development for this project.

Before starting the project, we posted our initial quick prototype in environmental Facebook groups to gauge interest and see the problems users had with current solutions. We got an amazing response from this original round of user research, about 500 people reached out to us to talk about what they wanted in a product like the one we were going to build. From this original round of user research, we identified that the main audience of this product would probably be millennials who were concerned about the future of the planet for their generation and for their children. Given this, we asked the question:

How might we help millennials easily track their carbon footprint and inspire them to reduce it by reassuring them that their actions will have a tangible impact?

After gathering this initial round of feedback from potential users, and learning what they disliked about the current solutions on the market, we were able to move forward confidently, knowing that we had an opportunity to solve a real problem for people.

Product Management Strategy

  1. Discovery and Market Research:

    Identified target audience through initial user outreach and analyzed existing solutions in the market to understand gaps and opportunities.

  2. User-Centric Problem Definition:

    Conducted in-depth interviews with potential customers to uncover pain points and needs related to carbon footprint tracking and reduction.

  3. Hypothesis and Value Proposition:

    Formulated a clear hypothesis for the product based on user insights and defined a unique value proposition to address the identified problem.

  4. MVP Development and Launch:

    Prioritized features for a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and coordinated with the development team to bring it to market quickly.

  5. Iterative Improvement:

    Established a feedback loop with early adopters, continuously gathered user data, and iterated on the product based on real-world usage and insights.

  6. Scaling and Growth:

    Developed strategies for user acquisition and retention, leading to over 40,000 active users and ongoing product improvements.

Solution

The LiveGreen app addresses the challenge of making carbon footprint tracking easy and engaging through several key features:

  • Track daily emissions: Users can monitor their emissions from all main categories including food, travel, purchases, and utilities.
  • Reduce carbon footprint: The app provides personalized daily tips based on the user's profile to help them reduce their carbon footprint.
  • Offset emissions: Users can offset their emissions by planting trees in reforestation projects.
  • Connect with others: The app allows users to connect with like-minded individuals who are also taking climate action.

The app's gamification features make it engaging to track and reduce daily personal emissions:

  • Simplifying users' emissions data into an eco score from 0 to 100
  • Rewarding points for taking personalized actions to reduce emissions
  • Creating a sense of community by displaying pictures of users completing their actions in a social feed
